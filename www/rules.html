Blackjack Rules

Objective: Beat the dealer by having a hand total closer to 21 than the dealer’s without exceeding 21 (busting).

Card Values

  • Number cards (2–10): Face value
  • Face cards (Jack, Queen, King): 10
  • Ace: 1 or 11 (player chooses what is most advantageous without busting)

Blackjack (Natural): An Ace + 10-value card on the initial two cards. Strongest hand, usually pays 3:2 (some tables pay 6:5).

Table Setup & Dealing

  • Dealer faces 5–9 player positions behind a semicircular table.
  • 1–8 standard 52-card decks are shuffled together.
  • Players place bets in the betting box.
  • Each betting position receives two cards (face-up or face-down depending on game type).
  • Dealer gets one card face-up and (in most games) a second card face-down (hole card).
  • If dealer’s up card is Ace or 10-value, they may peek for blackjack.

Player Decisions (on initial two cards)

  • Hit: Take another card.
  • Stand: Take no more cards.
  • Double Down: Double the bet and take exactly one more card.
  • Split: If pair of same rank, split into two hands (additional bet required). Each hand played separately. Rules on re-splitting/doubling after split vary.
  • Surrender: (where allowed) Forfeit half the bet and end hand immediately. Usually late surrender only.

After two cards, only Hit and Stand are available. Hand signals are required to avoid disputes.

Dealer Play

  • Dealer must hit until total is 17 or higher.
  • On soft 17 (17 with Ace counted as 11), dealer either hits or stands depending on table rules.
  • Dealer never doubles, splits, or surrenders.

Outcome & Payouts

  • If dealer busts, all remaining players win.
  • Otherwise, each hand is compared to dealer’s:
    • Higher total → Win (1:1 payout)
    • Lower total → Lose
    • Tie (push) → Bet returned
  • Player blackjack beats any non-blackjack 21 and pays 3:2 (unless dealer also has blackjack → push).

Insurance

When dealer shows Ace, players may make an insurance side bet (up to half original bet). Pays 2:1 if dealer has blackjack. Insurance is generally a poor bet long-term but can be advantageous with card counting.
