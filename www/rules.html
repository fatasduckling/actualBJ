Blackjack Rules
Complete Guide for Beginners & Players

Objective: Beat the dealer by getting a hand total closer to 21 than the dealer — without going over 21 (busting).

Card Values

  • Number cards (2–10): Worth their face value
  • Face cards (Jack, Queen, King): Worth 10
  • Ace: Worth 1 or 11 — whichever is most advantageous without busting

Blackjack (Natural): An Ace + any 10-value card on the initial deal. The strongest hand — usually pays 3:2 (some tables pay only 6:5).

Table Setup & Dealing

  • Dealer stands behind a semicircular table facing 5–7 player positions
  • Games use 1–8 standard 52-card decks shuffled together (shoe games common)
  • Players place bets in the betting circle before cards are dealt
  • Each player receives two cards (usually face-up in modern games)
  • Dealer gets one card face-up and one face-down (hole card)
  • If dealer’s upcard is Ace or 10-value, they may peek for blackjack

Player Decisions (after initial two cards)

  • Hit: Take another card to increase your total
  • Stand: Keep your current total — no more cards
  • Double Down: Double your original bet and receive exactly one more card
  • Split: If you have a pair of same-rank cards, split into two separate hands (additional bet required). Each hand is played independently. Re-splitting and doubling after split vary by casino.
  • Surrender (Late): (where allowed) Forfeit half your bet and end the hand immediately without playing it out

Important: After your initial two cards, only Hit and Stand are available. Always use hand signals to avoid disputes!

Dealer Play

  • Dealer must hit on any total of 16 or less
  • Dealer must stand on any total of 17 or higher
  • On soft 17 (17 with an Ace counted as 11), dealer either hits or stands depending on house rules (H17 = hits, S17 = stands)
  • Dealer never doubles, splits, or surrenders

Outcome & Payouts

  • If the dealer busts (over 21), all remaining players win
  • Otherwise, each player hand is compared to the dealer’s:
    • Higher total → You win even money (1:1 payout)
    • Lower total → You lose your bet
    • Equal total → Push (bet returned)
  • Player blackjack beats any dealer 21 made with more than two cards and pays 3:2 (or 6:5 at weaker tables)
  • If both player and dealer have blackjack → Push

Insurance Side Bet

When the dealer shows an Ace, players may place an insurance bet (up to half the original bet). Pays 2:1 if the dealer has blackjack.

Note: Insurance is generally a poor bet in basic play but becomes advantageous when card counting shows many 10s remaining.
